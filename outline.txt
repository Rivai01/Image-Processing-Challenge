Observations:

    - Detecting points seems like the obvious choice to find the center, especially in an image
    with no contamination or noise. However, upon adding contamination like in the example images, it seems
    like this could become faulty - a lot of things start to look like center points. So, I want to make sure that
    I'm looking for ways to detect the surrounding circles independently as well. Preliminary methods from looking
    at the OpenCV docs appear to include the Hough Transform, I will look into this more.

    - Perhaps a good way to approach the detection of the center would be to find all candidate locations within the image,
    then rank them using some criterion based on  their symmetry, darkness, etc.

Intermediate results:
    - Hough circle transform doesn't yield immediate results, it seems like this is likely to be 
    due to the fact that circles are cut off at the edges. Perhaps this could be circumvented by mirroring 
    the image at its edges?
    Setting the threshold parameters at 31, 31 does seem to at least find the center fairly well, but I'm sure 
    there are better methods.

    - I tried Canny edge detection, but this also seems not to apply that well to this situation, since 
    the circles are so blurry

    - The image has very clear oscillatory behavior, so perhaps taking a (polar) Fourier transform will be helpful. 
    It isn't exactly periodic, but finding the frequencies involved could be helpful - and even if I don't directly 
    end up using them for detection, they could be a good tool for verifying results.

    - Applying Gaussian sharpening filters does a good job of removing the blurriness around the rings
    
    - Finding contours of constant pixel values works extremely well
